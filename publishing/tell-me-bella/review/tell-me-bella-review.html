Foreword (M. K.) 5 issues

Typo

…He edited a news-paper in Tiflis in 1917-18…

→ newspaper — one word; the hyphen is a residual OCR line-break artefact preserved by --join-hyphens.

Punct

…he lost his father-a prosperous landowner and a high government official…

→ Hyphen used as a parenthetical dash. Hart's Rules: spaced en dash — his father — a prosperous landowner

Punct

…He edited a news-paper in Tiflis in 1917-18, wrote numerous articles…

→ Hyphen used for a range. Hart's Rules: unspaced en dash — 1917–18

Style

…wrote numerous articles, a novel, literary studies and other material…

→ Oxford comma missing before the final item in a list of four. literary studies, and other material

Query

…Life on the Old Roman Road… Scenes from an Armenian Childhood…

Book titles appear in plain roman type. In published prose these would normally be italicised. Should Markdown italic markup be added — e.g. *Life on the Old Roman Road*? Confirm with editor whether this is intentional for the current workflow.

Pale-blue Flowers 7 issues

Punct

…the priest and the beadle—the priest had arranged for the clerk…

→ Em dash with no spaces. Hart's Rules: spaced en dash — beadle — the

Punct

…in spite of their fervent efforts-they had lit countless candles in front of pictures of saints-they had not produced any children…

→ Hyphens used as parenthetical dashes (twice). Hart's Rules: spaced en dashes — efforts — they … saints — they

Query

…he could never reconcile with the thought that a son of his own…

Unusual construction. The standard idiom is reconcile himself to or come to terms with. Without the reflexive pronoun the phrasing reads oddly — possible translator's idiom. Confirm with editor.

Style

…until a snow-flake landed on his eye-lashes…

→ Both are standard closed forms in modern British English: snowflake, eyelashes. These hyphens may reflect period usage in the original translation — confirm with editor if you wish to preserve the 1960s flavour.

Query

…if he came across a bear-show, he would stop and watch it…

Unusual compound. Bear show (open) or bear-baiting show might read more naturally to a modern audience. Possible translator's idiom or period usage — confirm with editor.
